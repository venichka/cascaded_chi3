# Cascaded $\chi^{(2)}{:}\chi^{(2)} \to \chi^{(3)}_\text{eff}$ in a Doubly-Resonant Dielectric Metasurface

A numerical companion to `Cascaded_chi3_metasurface_note.md/.tex`. Twelve figures generated by the modular simulation in this directory, each demonstrating one phenomenon predicted by the temporal coupled-mode theory (TCMT) of Section 4.

---

## 1. Theoretical setup (one-page summary)

The metasurface supports two co-localised resonances:

- **FM** at $\omega_1$ — quasi-BIC with controlled radiative coupling $\gamma_1^{(\text{rad})}$.
- **SH** at $\omega_2 \approx 2\omega_1$ — symmetry-protected BIC (or under-coupled qBIC), $\gamma_2 \simeq \gamma_2^{(\text{nr})}$, large $Q_2 = \omega_2/\gamma_2$.

In the rotating frame at the pump frequency $\omega_p$ (with $\delta_p = \omega_p - \omega_1$, $\Delta\omega = 2\omega_1 - \omega_2$, $\Delta_{\rm SH} = 2\delta_p + \Delta\omega$):

$$
\boxed{\;\frac{da_1}{dt} = \bigl(i\delta_p - \tfrac{\gamma_1}{2}\bigr) a_1
                        + i\beta^{*} a_1^{*} a_2
                        + i\alpha_3 |a_1|^2 a_1
                        + \sqrt{\gamma_1^{(\text{rad})}} \, s_+\;}
$$

$$
\boxed{\;\frac{da_2}{dt} = \bigl(i\Delta_{\rm SH} - \tfrac{\gamma_2}{2}\bigr) a_2
                        + i\beta\, a_1^{2}\;}
$$

with the input–output relation $s_- = -s_+ + \sqrt{\gamma_1^{(\text{rad})}}\, a_1$.

**Tensor coupling (note eq. (beta))**:
$$
\beta = \frac{\varepsilon_0 \omega_1}{4} \int \chi^{(2)}_{ijk}(\mathbf{r})\, f_{2,i}^{*}(\mathbf{r})\, f_{1,j}(\mathbf{r})\, f_{1,k}(\mathbf{r})\, d^3 r.
$$

**Adiabatic elimination of the SH** ($|\Delta\omega| \gg \gamma_2/2$, $|\dot a_2| \ll |\Delta\omega \, a_2|$): set $da_2/dt = 0$,

$$
a_2 = \frac{i\beta\, a_1^{2}}{\gamma_2/2 - i\Delta_{\rm SH}}.
$$

Substituting into the FM equation gives the effective Kerr-like nonlinearity:

$$
\boxed{\;\alpha_3^{\text{eff,casc}} = -\frac{|\beta|^2 \, \Delta_{\rm SH}}{\Delta_{\rm SH}^2 + (\gamma_2/2)^2}\;}
\qquad
\boxed{\;\gamma_{2\text{PA}}^{\text{eff}} = \frac{|\beta|^2 \, (\gamma_2/2)}{\Delta_{\rm SH}^2 + (\gamma_2/2)^2}\;}
$$

with the simple far-detuned form $\alpha_3^{\text{eff,casc}} \to -|\beta|^2 / \Delta_{\rm SH}$.

**Final boxed cascaded susceptibility (note eq. (chi3casc_explicit))**:

$$
\boxed{\;\chi^{(3)}_{\text{casc}} \;\sim\; -\frac{\omega_1}{6\, n_2^{\,2}\, \Delta\omega}\, |\chi^{(2)}|^2\, \eta_{\text{geom}}\;}
$$

with $\eta_{\text{geom}}\sim 0.01\!-\!0.5$ a purely geometric overlap. Only $n_2$ (the SH-mode effective index) survives — the four powers of $n_1$ cancel between numerator and denominator.

**Dimensionless units used in the simulations**: $\gamma_2 = 1$, $\beta = 1$. Then time is in units of $1/\gamma_2$, mode amplitudes in units of $\gamma_2/|\beta|$, and `delta1`, `Domega`, `gamma1`, `gamma1_rad`, `alpha3`, `|s+|^2` are all dimensionless.

## 2. Validation and basic phenomena

### 2.1 Validation: TCMT vs analytic effective coefficients

![[figures/validation.png]]

**Setup.** Drive the FM on resonance ($\delta_p = 0$) at small input ($|s_+| = 0.01$). Sweep $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 \in [-30, 30]$. At each detuning, integrate to steady state; extract the cascaded contribution from

$$
C \equiv \frac{i\, a_1^{*} a_2}{a_1\, |a_1|^2}
= -\frac{1}{\gamma_2/2 - i\Delta\omega},\qquad
\alpha_3^{\text{eff,casc}}_{\rm num} = \mathrm{Im}(C),\quad
\gamma_{2\text{PA}}^{\rm eff}_{\rm num} = -\mathrm{Re}(C).
$$

**Panel (a)** — Cascaded Kerr (real). The numerical TCMT (open circles) sits exactly on the analytic full form $-\Delta\omega / (\Delta\omega^2 + 1/4)$ (black solid). The simple $-1/\Delta\omega$ form (grey dashed) deviates as $|\Delta\omega| \to \gamma_2/2$ — the adiabatic-elimination breakdown the note flags in Section 5(i) and Section 10(1).

**Panel (b)** — Cascaded two-photon-like loss. A Lorentzian peaked at $\Delta\omega = 0$ with FWHM $= \gamma_2$. This loss is unavoidable: the price of resonant enhancement, intensity-dependent, with no analogue in $\mathrm{Im}\,\chi^{(3)}$ — it is set entirely by $\gamma_2$.

**Takeaway.** TCMT is correct to the 4-th decimal; the simple "$-1/\Delta\omega$" form quoted in many cascading papers underestimates the true coefficient by 70 % at $\Delta\omega = \gamma_2/2$ and the difference grows fast as you approach resonance.

### 2.2 Sign tunability — walking $\Delta\omega$ through zero

![[figures/sign_flip.png]]

**Setup.** Sweep $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 \in [-30, 30]$ at small input (small-signal regime). Compute $\alpha_3^{\rm eff,total} = \alpha_3 + \alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc}(\Delta\omega)$ for two bare-Kerr scenarios: $\widetilde\alpha_3 = 0$ (pure cascading) and $\widetilde\alpha_3 = +0.05$ (with native bulk Kerr).

**Why it matters.** The cascaded Kerr is *odd* in $\Delta\omega$:

$$
\alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc}(-\Delta\omega) = -\alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc}(\Delta\omega).
$$

Walking the SH resonance through $2\omega_1$ — by tuning the meta-atom geometry, applying strain, or thermal tuning — flips the sign of the effective Kerr response from self-defocusing ($\Delta\omega > 0$) to self-focusing ($\Delta\omega < 0$). With a non-zero bare Kerr added, the zero of the total response shifts to $\Delta\omega = 1/\widetilde\alpha_3$ (red curve, zero at $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 = 20$).

**Grey shaded band** $|\Delta\omega| \le \gamma_2$: adiabatic-elimination breakdown region (Section 10(1)).

**Takeaway.** A *single* metasurface can deliver self-focusing, self-defocusing, *or* cancel native Kerr exactly, by choice of $\Delta\omega$. This is an experimentally accessible knob — historically exploited in soliton compression [DeSalvo 1992, Liu 1999].

### 2.3 Bandwidth–enhancement sum rule

![[figures/sum_rule.png]]

**Setup.** Fix $\Delta\omega$ in physical units; vary $Q_2 = \omega_2/\gamma_2$ via $\gamma_2$. Plot the modulation transfer function $|\kappa_{\rm casc}(\Omega)|$ on a *shared* $\Omega$ grid for $Q_2 \in \{10^3, 4\!\times\!10^3, 16\!\times\!10^3\}$.

**Derivation (Section 6).** $\kappa_{\rm casc}(\Omega) = -|\beta|^2 / [\gamma_2/2 - i(\Omega + \Delta_{\rm SH})]$. Setting $\Delta_{\rm SH} = \gamma_2/2$ (bandwidth-optimal):
- peak $|\kappa| = |\beta|^2/(\gamma_2/2) \propto Q_2$,
- FWHM $= \sqrt{3}\,\gamma_2 \propto 1/Q_2$,
- peak $\times$ FWHM $= 2\sqrt{3}\,|\beta|^2$ — **independent of $Q_2$**.

**Panel (a)** — Lorentzian shapes line up at $\Omega_{\rm peak} = -\Delta_{\rm SH}$. As $Q_2$ grows from 1 k to 16 k, the peak grows by 16× and the FWHM shrinks by 16×; area conserved.

**Panel (b)** — Numerical verification. Peak ratios `[1, 4, 16]`, FWHM ratios `[1, 1/4, 1/16]`, product ratios `[1, 1, 1]` to better than $2\!\times\!10^{-4}$.

**Takeaway.** Resonant enhancement *redistributes* nonlinear action; it does not increase the area under the curve. This is the same rule that limits oscillator-strength sums in linear absorption — purely classical, unbreakable by clever resonator design.

## 3. Cascaded-induced FM dynamics

### 3.1 Bistability — the first experimental fingerprint at the FM

![[figures/bistability.png]]

**Setup.** Parameters chosen to push the bistability cusp well clear of the parametric threshold: $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 = 5$, $\gamma_1/\gamma_2 = 0.4$, $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1 = 0.5$, blue-detuned pump $\delta_p/\gamma_2 = 0.8$. Stepwise quasi-static sweep: at each input level, integrate the full TCMT to steady state initialised from the previous step's state.

**Why blue detuning?** With $\Delta\omega > 0$, $\alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc} < 0$ — the effective FM resonance shifts *down* with intensity. To get hysteresis, the pump must sit *above* the cold-cavity resonance ($\delta_p > 0$); as input grows, the shifted resonance climbs toward the pump, the response runs away, and the system snaps to the upper branch.

**Bistability cusp condition.** From the cubic equation $|a_1|^2 [(g_1 + \gamma_e |a_1|^2)^2 + (\delta_p + \alpha_e |a_1|^2)^2] = \gamma_1^{(\rm rad)} |s_+|^2$, the saddle-node fold opens for $\delta_p > \sqrt{3}\,\gamma_1/2$ (here $4\gamma_1/2 \gg \sqrt{3}\,\gamma_1/2$, comfortably bistable).

**Result.** Snap-up at $|s_+|^2 \approx 3.0$, snap-down at $|s_+|^2 \approx 1.7$, both sitting *exactly* on the analytic Kerr+2PA saddle-nodes (grey curves). The SH amplitude (panel b) jumps in lockstep with the FM — both mode amplitudes are tied by $a_2 = i\beta a_1^2 / (\gamma_2/2 - i\Delta_{\rm SH})$.

**Takeaway.** A *purely monochromatic* CW pump can drive the FM through bistability via cascaded SPM. This is the cleanest experimental signature of cascaded $\chi^{(3)}$ at the FM port — far more sensitive than reflectance measurements at the SH wavelength.

### 3.2 Saturation and pump depletion

![[figures/saturation.png]]

**Setup.** Realistic LiNbO₃ design point from Section 9: $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 = 2$, $\gamma_1/\gamma_2 = 10$, $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1 = 0.5$. Pump on FM resonance; sweep $|s_+|^2$ up to the parametric-oscillation threshold (sat parameter $|\beta a_1|/|\Delta\omega| \approx 0.9$).

**Panel (a)** — log–log of intracavity energies. Linear-cavity reference: $|a_{1,\rm lin}|^2 = \gamma_1^{(\rm rad)} |s_+|^2 / (\gamma_1/2)^2$. The full-TCMT $|a_1|^2$ tracks the linear at small signal then peels below it as cascaded 2PA loss drains FM into SH. The Kerr-only cubic (black thin) sits *exactly* on the TCMT (open markers) — confirming that at any deterministic steady state the algebraic substitution $a_2 = i\beta a_1^2/(\gamma_2/2 - i\Delta_{\rm SH})$ holds. The SH energy crosses the FM near $|s_+|^2 \approx 30$.

**Panel (b)** — Linear-x view. SH energy fraction $|a_2|^2/(|a_1|^2 + |a_2|^2)$ (red, left axis) and saturation parameter (green, right axis) climb together. Both show the characteristic log-like rolling shape on a linear axis (asymptotically $|a_1|^2 \propto |s_+|^{2/3}$, so sat $\propto |s_+|^{1/3}$).

**Why the cap at sat $\approx 1$?** Past $|\beta a_1| \gtrsim |\Delta\omega|$ the trivial fixed point becomes parametrically unstable and the deterministic ODE enters a limit cycle (see §3.3). Below threshold, Kerr-only cubic and full TCMT are equivalent to machine precision.

**Takeaway.** The Kerr-equivalent description holds up to $\lvert\beta a_1\rvert \approx \lvert\Delta\omega\rvert$. Beyond, the FM sees a non-perturbative back-action — useful for parametric oscillation (next figure), bad for "the system as a clean Kerr medium". Maximum useful $|a_1|^2$ scales as $1/Q_2$ at fixed $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2$ — **high-Q devices saturate at lower powers**.

### 3.3 Parametric-oscillation onset above threshold

![[figures/parametric_onset.png]]

**Setup.** Lighter FM damping than the realistic design point ($\gamma_1/\gamma_2 = 2$, so the threshold sits at numerically tractable input levels). Drive CW; seed $a_2(0) = a_{2,\rm trivial} + 10^{-3} i$ (a small imaginary perturbation off the trivial fixed point). Integrate for 100 SH lifetimes.

**Result.**
- **sat = 0.7** (below threshold, blue): perturbation decays to trivial FP.
- **sat = 1.0** (at threshold, green): settles at trivial FP $|a_2|^2 \approx 3.8$.
- **sat = 1.3** (above threshold, red): trajectory enters a clear limit cycle, $|a_2|^2 \in [10, 100]$.
- **sat = 1.7** (deep above, orange): even larger excursions, $|a_2|^2 \in [10, 200]$.

**Interpretation.** Above the parametric threshold the trivial FP $a_2 = i\beta a_1^2/(\gamma_2/2 - i\Delta_{\rm SH})$ has at least one positive eigenvalue under linearisation — any seed with non-zero projection on the unstable mode grows exponentially until the nonlinearity clamps it on a limit cycle. This is the analogue of the optical parametric oscillator threshold the note hints at in Section 10(2). The deterministic "fixed point" the saturation figure plots is *metastable*; in noise it would also leave that branch and go onto this limit cycle.

**Takeaway.** $|\beta a_1| \approx |\Delta\omega|$ is a hard ceiling for the cascaded-Kerr description — not because the cubic equation diverges (it does not), but because the deterministic fixed point loses stability and the system runs off to a parametric oscillator regime that no Kerr medium can model.

## 4. Time-domain consequences

### 4.1 Pulse-bandwidth limit

![[figures/pulse_bandwidth.png]]

**Setup.** Drive the FM with a Gaussian pump pulse $s_+(t) = s_0 \exp\!\bigl[-(t-t_0)^2/(2\sigma_t^2)\bigr]$ at the FM carrier ($\delta_p = 0$, $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 = 2$). Vary $\sigma_t$ from 0.05 to 30 SH lifetimes.

**Adiabatic prediction.** Whenever the SH stays slaved to the FM, $|a_2(t)|^2 = |a_1(t)|^4 / (\Delta\omega^2 + (\gamma_2/2)^2)$.

**Panel (a)** — Short pulse $\sigma_t \gamma_2 = 0.3$. The SH (red) lags and undershoots the adiabatic prediction (blue dotted). After the pulse, the SH rings down at rate $\gamma_2/2$ — the imprint of the SH cavity memory.

**Panel (b)** — Long pulse $\sigma_t \gamma_2 = 8$. SH and adiabatic prediction overlap perfectly: the SH faithfully tracks $|a_1|^2$ in real time.

**Panel (c)** — Tracking quality at the moment of FM peak, $R = |a_2|^2 / |a_{2,\rm adiab}|^2$. Asymptote $R \to 1$ for $\sigma_t \gamma_2 \gg 1$, drops to $\sim 0.05$ for $\sigma_t \gamma_2 \ll 1$. Crossover sits exactly at $\sigma_t \gamma_2 = 1$ — the time-domain face of the bandwidth–enhancement sum rule of §2.3.

**Takeaway.** Cascaded $\chi^{(3)}$ enhancement is not "fast" Kerr in the ultrafast sense. For pulse durations shorter than $\sim 1/\gamma_2$, the SH cannot follow and the cascaded contribution rolls off. With $Q_2 = 4{,}000$, $1/\gamma_2 \approx 1.7$ ps — anything sub-picosecond is too fast.

### 4.2 Manley–Rowe / energy conservation check

![[figures/manley_rowe.png]]

**Setup.** Lossless TCMT ($\gamma_1 = \gamma_2 = 0$, no input). IC $(a_1, a_2) = (1, 0)$, $\Delta\omega = 0$ (degenerate, fastest exchange). Compare two RHS: correct DFG factor (left) vs halved DFG (right, the "$\kappa$ vs $\kappa/2$" mismatch the note flags as the original draft's bug).

**Why this matters.** Manley–Rowe in the lossless limit demands

$$
\frac{d}{dt}\bigl(|a_1|^2 + |a_2|^2\bigr) = 0 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta^{(1)} = \beta^{*},
$$

i.e., the FM-side coupling is the complex-conjugate of the SH-side coupling — a *single* constant $\beta$, not two unrelated ones. This is built in by the factor of 2 in the DFG polarisation $P^{(2)}(\omega_1) = 2\varepsilon_0 \chi^{(2)} E(2\omega_1) E^*(\omega_1)$ (Section 2 of the note).

**Result.**
- **Correct** (left): total energy stays at 1.0 to machine precision (drift $1.4 \times 10^{-14}$).
- **Halved DFG** (right): total energy grows past 1.6 by $t = 2/\beta$ (drift = 1.0). Energy is created out of nothing.

**Takeaway.** The factor-of-2 the note rederives is not a cosmetic fix — without it, the model violates the second law. Any TCMT code that drops it is silently wrong; this figure is the smoke test.

## 5. Design space

### 5.1 Bulk PPLN vs metasurface — same sum rule, different operating point

![[figures/bulk_vs_metasurface.png]]

**Setup.** Plot $|\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc}|/|\chi^{(3)}_{\rm native}|$ vs usable bandwidth $\Delta f$ (Hz) for both regimes in LiNbO₃:

- **Metasurface** (eq. (chi3casc_explicit)): enhancement $= (Q_2/(12\,n_2^2))\,\eta_{\rm geom}\, |\chi^{(2)}|^2/|\chi^{(3)}|_{\rm native}$, bandwidth $= \gamma_2/(2\pi) = 2\omega_1/(2\pi Q_2)$.
- **Bulk phase-mismatched PPLN** (Section 9): $\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc} \sim 2|\chi^{(2)}|^2/(\Delta k\, L)$, bandwidth $\sim |\Delta k|\,v_g/(2\pi) \sim 1$ THz.

**Sum-rule hyperbola.** Both regimes obey peak $\times$ BW $= $ const. With LiNbO₃ numbers, the metasurface points trace the line `enh × BW(Hz) = 2.37 × 10¹²` exactly. The bulk PPLN at $\Delta k\, L = 1$ gives `enh = 2.5` at $\Delta f = 1$ THz, sitting essentially on the same hyperbola.

**Trade-off.** The metasurface trades roughly 1–2 orders of magnitude of $|\chi^{(3)}|$ for ~4 orders of magnitude of bandwidth, relative to bulk PPLN. Both regimes are "best in class" for their respective applications:
- **High-Q metasurface**: CW-pumped bistability, parametric oscillation thresholds, frequency-comb stabilisation, narrowband switching.
- **Bulk phase-mismatched PPLN**: ultrafast soliton compression, broadband all-optical switching.

### 5.2 The geometric overlap $\eta_{\rm geom}$ is the binding constraint

![[figures/overlap_eta.png]]

**Setup.** Plot enhancement vs $\eta_{\rm geom}$ at $Q_2 \in \{4{,}000, 10{,}000\}$ on log–log.

**The overlap integral.** From eq. (beta):
$$
\eta_{\rm geom} \propto \biggl|\int \chi^{(2)}_{ijk}(\mathbf{r})\, f_{2,i}^{*}\, f_{1,j}\, f_{1,k}\, d^3 r\biggr|^2 / (\text{normalisations}).
$$

This integral *vanishes* unless three conditions hold (Section 7):

1. **No bulk inversion symmetry** in the meta-atom material (LiNbO₃, GaAs, GaP, AlGaAs — fine).
2. **Mode-symmetry mismatch must be lifted.** If both modes have the same parity under all shared spatial symmetries, the integrand has a definite parity that may force the integral to zero. Quasi-BIC engineering deliberately breaks one such symmetry while preserving high $Q_2$.
3. **Phase-matching at the unit-cell scale.** Even with non-zero tensor and mismatched parities, a poor radial overlap of $f_2$ with $f_1^2$ kills the result.

**Regimes.**
- **Random mode pairing** ($\eta \sim 10^{-3}$, red shaded): cascaded enhancement *below* native, even at $Q_2 = 10{,}000$. The metasurface is *worse* than bulk LiNbO₃.
- **Engineered qBIC pair** ($\eta \sim 0.3$, green shaded): the note's design point. $Q_2 = 4{,}000$ gives 25×, $Q_2 = 10{,}000$ gives 60×.
- **Perfect overlap** ($\eta = 1$): hard upper bound; geometric constraints make this unachievable in practice.

**Takeaway.** $Q_2$ alone buys *nothing* if $\eta_{\rm geom}$ is small. A factor-of-2 increase in $Q_2$ shifts the line by ~0.4 dex; a factor-of-100 reduction in $\eta_{\rm geom}$ shifts it by 2 dex *down*. Geometry first; resonance Q second.

### 5.3 Refractive-index dependence: $1/n_2^2$, not $1/n^3$

![[figures/n2_scaling.png]]

**Setup.** Plot $|\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc}|$ vs SH-mode index $n_2$ at fixed $Q_2 = 4{,}000$, $\eta_{\rm geom} = 0.3$. Compare:
- correct: $\propto 1/n_2^2$ (note's eq. (chi3casc_explicit)).
- naive plane-wave: $\propto 1/\bar{n}^3$ with $\bar{n} = (n_1 + n_2)/2$.

**Why $n_1$ cancels.** The $f_2{:}f_1 f_1$ overlap integral has dimension `~ |chi^(2)| / (eps_0^{3/2} n_2 n_1^2 sqrt(V_eff))`. The $\int |f_1|^4 d^3r$ in the denominator scales as `~ 1/(eps_0^2 n_1^4 V_eff)`. Their ratio leaves only $n_2^2$. Physically, modes in higher-index material concentrate *less* field per unit stored energy, so the $f_2{:}f_1 f_1$ coupling is suppressed by $1/n_2$, but the FM-mode normalisation symmetrically sucks power off both ends.

**Result.** At LiNbO₃ ($n_1 = 2.21$, $n_2 = 2.26$): correct/naive ratio = 2.19, matching the exact analytical $\bar{n}^3/n_2^2 = (2.235)^3/(2.26)^2 = 2.18$. The naïve formula underestimates by ~2× at telecom indices; for higher-index materials (Si: $n \approx 3.5$) the underestimate is ~3×.

**Takeaway.** A non-trivial correction over the original derivation; particularly important when comparing with experiment, where measured $\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc}$ rarely matches the "obvious" $1/n^3$ scaling.

### 5.4 Power-resolved transmission — the experimental observable

![[figures/transmission_lineshape.png]]

**Setup.** Sweep pump frequency $\delta_p/\gamma_2 \in [-15, 15]$ at three input powers (low / moderate / high). Plot $|s_-/s_+|^2$, the FM-port transmission. Realistic LiNbO₃ design point: $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2 = 2$, $\gamma_1/\gamma_2 = 10$, $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1 = 0.5$ (critically coupled at $\delta_p = 0$).

**Linear (small-signal) response.** Critically-coupled Lorentzian dip to 0 at $\delta_p = 0$, FWHM $= \gamma_1$. (Blue curve — verifies the linear cavity numerics.)

**Higher-power lineshape.** Two competing effects:
1. **Self-detuning from cascaded Kerr.** With $\alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc} < 0$ (at $\Delta_{\rm SH} > 0$), the effective FM resonance walks blue with intensity. The dip pulls toward positive $\delta_p$ on the side where $\Delta_{\rm SH} > 0$.
2. **Sign flip of $\alpha_3^{\rm eff,casc}$ across $\Delta_{\rm SH} = 0$.** Sweeping $\delta_p$ also sweeps $\Delta_{\rm SH} = 2\delta_p + \Delta\omega$ — at $\delta_p = -\Delta\omega/2 = -1$ the cascaded coefficient changes sign and the response goes from self-defocusing to self-focusing. The high-power red curve has *two* dips (one on each side of the $\Delta_{\rm SH} = 0$ point) and a transmission peak in between — uniquely a doubly-resonant signature.

**Takeaway.** For practical metrology of cascaded $\chi^{(3)}$, a power-swept transmission scan is the most diagnostic single experiment: low-power Lorentzian gives $\gamma_1$ and $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1$; high-power lineshape encodes $\beta$, $\Delta\omega$, and the sign-flip-induced double dip. *And* you can read the bistability cusp directly off the same scan.

## 7. Recommendations for metasurface design

### 7.1 Hierarchy of important parameters

In order of decreasing leverage on $|\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc}|$:

1. **$\eta_{\rm geom}$** — three orders of magnitude separating poor and engineered overlap. *Get this first.* No amount of $Q_2$ rescues a vanishing tensor integral.
2. **$|\chi^{(2)}|$ of the host material** — enters squared. Switching from GaAs to LiNbO₃ to AlGaAs is ~factor 2; switching from a non-2nd-order material to LiNbO₃ is infinite.
3. **$Q_2/n_2^2$** — `1/n_2^2` is fixed by material; $Q_2$ is engineered. A factor 2 in $Q_2$ buys factor 2 in peak enhancement, factor 1/2 in bandwidth.
4. **$\Delta\omega/\gamma_2$** — operating point. The bandwidth-optimal point is $\Delta\omega \sim \gamma_2/2$; far-detuned operation buys more pump-power headroom (saturation pushed up) at the cost of lower peak enhancement.
5. **$\gamma_1/\gamma_2$ and $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1$** — port coupling. Sets the input efficiency; affects bistability threshold but not the cascaded coefficient itself.

### 7.2 Recommended design sequence

1. **Pick a non-centrosymmetric host** with the largest $|\chi^{(2)}|$ compatible with low loss in the FM and SH bands. LiNbO₃ at telecom is the standard; AlGaAs at near-IR has higher $|\chi^{(2)}|$ but higher absorption near the band edge.
2. **Engineer the qBIC pair to maximise $\eta_{\rm geom}$.** Use full-wave eigenmode simulation (COMSOL/MEEP) to compute the tensor overlap integral for candidate unit cells. Target $\eta_{\rm geom} \gtrsim 0.1$. Use periodic poling or geometry asymmetry to lift mode-parity constraints. Verify $\eta_{\rm geom}$ stays high under realistic fabrication tolerances (lithography errors, sidewall angle).
3. **Set $Q_2$ from the application's bandwidth requirement.** $Q_2 \in [10^3, 10^5]$ corresponds to $\Delta f \in [400$ GHz, 4 GHz$]$. Higher $Q_2$ → more enhancement, less bandwidth, lower saturation power.
4. **Set $\Delta\omega$ near the bandwidth-optimal point** $\Delta\omega \sim \gamma_2$ for max enhancement, or further off-resonance ($\Delta\omega \gtrsim 5\gamma_2$) for higher saturation power and sign-tunability through $\Delta\omega = 0$.
5. **Set $Q_1$ for input efficiency.** $\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)}/\gamma_1 \sim 0.5$ (critically coupled) gives best dip depth; under-coupled FM is fine if you have a strong source. Avoid over-coupled FM ($\gamma_1^{(\rm rad)} > \gamma_1$) — wastes input.
6. **Verify pump power requirement** does not exceed material damage thresholds. Saturation pump intensity scales as $1/\sqrt{Q_2}$ at fixed $\Delta\omega/\gamma_2$ — high-Q designs saturate at low intensity. With $Q_2 = 10^4$ in LiNbO₃, several MW/cm² in the resonator suffices for saturation, well within damage limits.

### 7.3 Trade-offs and optimisations

| You want… | Trade-off | How to mitigate |
|---|---|---|
| Higher $|\chi^{(3)}_{\rm casc}|$ | Narrower BW (sum rule); lower saturation; more thermal load per photon | Use higher-Q SH; couple to active cooling; choose low-loss host (LiNbO₃) |
| Wider BW | Lower peak; need more pump for same nonlinear phase | Lower $Q_2$, or mode-engineer for moderate $\eta_{\rm geom}$ at low Q |
| Faster temporal response | Lose all cascaded enhancement at $\sigma_t \gamma_2 \lesssim 1$ | Switch to bulk phase-mismatched PPLN regime |
| Sign tunability without device swap | Need fine $\Delta\omega$ control | Strain or thermo-optic tuning of the SH mode (possible in LiNbO₃ thin films) |
| Avoid parametric oscillation | Keep operating well below $|\beta a_1| \approx |\Delta\omega|$ | Larger $\Delta\omega$, lower input — at the cost of lower phase shift |
| Avoid thermo-optic / free-carrier confusion | Loss leads to thermal heating | Use LiNbO₃ (low absorption) over GaAs or Si near the band edge |
| Suppress cascaded TPA | TPA peaks at $\Delta_{\rm SH} = 0$; finite at any $\Delta\omega$ | Operate at $|\Delta\omega| \gtrsim 2\gamma_2$ where the imaginary part is suppressed by $1/\Delta_{\rm SH}^2$ |

### 7.4 Things that look like good ideas but are not

- **"Just push $Q_2$ to $10^6$ to get $1000\times$ enhancement."** Two reasons this fails:
  1. *Saturation* — pump-power threshold drops as $1/\sqrt{Q_2}$, so you can't reach the peak enhancement at any practical input.
  2. *Linewidth ↔ fabrication tolerances* — at $Q_2 = 10^6$, the SH resonance has a $40$ MHz linewidth; sub-nm fabrication errors detune resonators randomly across the array, smearing the metasurface response back to a low-Q effective Q.
- **"Use the bulk Kerr too — additive."** Yes, but the bulk $|\chi^{(3)}|$ in LiNbO₃ is $\sim 2 \times 10^{-21}$ m²/V², 25× below the cascaded value. The bulk contribution is a few-percent correction at the design point. It only matters near the zero-crossing of the total Kerr, where it shifts the zero (`fig_sign_flip` red curve).
- **"Add gain to push past the OPO threshold."** Above threshold the system *is* an OPO, not a Kerr medium. Useful for parametric source design but not for the use case the note targets (cascaded χ³ for low-threshold bistability, frequency-comb interactions, etc.).

## 8. Open issues / not yet simulated

- **Tensor structure of $\chi^{(2)}_{ijk}$.** Our simulations use a scalar $\beta$. A real metasurface has a tensorial $\chi^{(2)}_{ijk}$ contracted with the vectorial mode profiles. Different polarisation channels give different $\beta$; the signs and magnitudes can be very different across channels. Full-wave simulation is needed for a quantitative number.
- **Disorder.** Lithographic disorder broadens $\gamma_2$ inhomogeneously. The TCMT model assumes a single resonator; for a metasurface array, the response is a convolution.
- **Thermo-optic and free-carrier.** Section 10(4) flags these as easy to confuse with Kerr at the intensities required (~MW/cm²). Not modelled here.
- **Vector mode profiles in $\eta_{\rm geom}$.** The figure `overlap_eta` treats $\eta_{\rm geom}$ as a free parameter; the actual value comes from a full-wave eigenmode integration that we have not done.
